## Search Ads (Google Ads / Microsoft Ads)

| Metric | Industry Average | Good | Excellent |
|--------|-----------------|------|-----------|
| Click-through rate (CTR) | 3.2–4.0% | 5–7% | 8%+ |
| Cost per click (CPC) | £1.50–£3.00 | £0.80–£1.50 | < £0.80 |
| Conversion rate | 3.5–5.0% | 6–8% | 10%+ |
| Quality Score | 5–6/10 | 7–8/10 | 9–10/10 |
| Cost per acquisition (CPA) | £30–£75 | £15–£30 | < £15 |
| Impression share | 40–60% | 60–80% | 80%+ |

**Quality Score breakdown:** Expected CTR, ad relevance, and landing page experience each contribute roughly equally. A score below 5 on any component signals a specific problem worth investigating.

## Social Ads (Meta, LinkedIn, TikTok)

| Metric | Meta (avg) | LinkedIn (avg) | TikTok (avg) |
|--------|-----------|----------------|--------------|
| CTR | 0.9–1.5% | 0.4–0.6% | 1.0–2.0% |
| CPM (cost per 1,000 impressions) | £6–£12 | £25–£45 | £4–£10 |
| Engagement rate | 1.5–3.0% | 0.5–1.0% | 3.0–6.0% |
| Video completion rate (15s) | 25–35% | 20–30% | 35–50% |
| Video completion rate (30s) | 15–20% | 12–18% | 20–30% |
| Cost per engagement | £0.10–£0.30 | £0.50–£1.50 | £0.05–£0.20 |
| Cost per lead (lead gen forms) | £8–£25 | £30–£80 | £5–£15 |

**Platform notes:**
- LinkedIn CPMs are significantly higher, but B2B lead quality often justifies the cost. Evaluate on cost-per-qualified-lead, not CPM alone.
- TikTok engagement rates are inflated by passive interactions (views, auto-plays). Weight comments and shares more heavily.
- Meta's algorithm rewards creative refresh — expect performance decay after 7–14 days on the same creative.

## Display Ads (Google Display Network, Programmatic)

| Metric | Industry Average | Good | Excellent |
|--------|-----------------|------|-----------|
| CTR | 0.35–0.50% | 0.50–0.80% | 1.0%+ |
| Viewability rate | 50–60% | 65–75% | 80%+ |
| CPM | £2–£6 | £1–£2 (with targeting) | Context-dependent |
| View-through conversion rate | 0.1–0.3% | 0.3–0.5% | 0.5%+ |
| Banner blindness drop-off | 40–50% after 3 exposures | — | — |

**Viewability matters more than CTR for display.** A campaign with 0.3% CTR but 80% viewability is outperforming one with 0.5% CTR and 45% viewability — the second is likely counting accidental clicks from poor placements.

## Email Marketing

| Metric | B2B Average | B2C Average | Good (either) | Cause for concern |
|--------|------------|------------|----------------|-------------------|
| Open rate | 20–25% | 15–22% | 30%+ | < 12% |
| Click-through rate | 2.5–3.5% | 1.5–2.5% | 4%+ | < 1% |
| Click-to-open rate (CTOR) | 12–15% | 8–12% | 18%+ | < 6% |
| Unsubscribe rate | < 0.3% | < 0.5% | < 0.1% | > 0.5% per send |
| Bounce rate (hard) | < 0.5% | < 0.5% | < 0.2% | > 2% (list hygiene issue) |
| Spam complaint rate | < 0.1% | < 0.1% | < 0.02% | > 0.1% (deliverability risk) |

**Note on open rates:** Apple Mail Privacy Protection inflates open rates for Apple Mail users (roughly 50% of consumer email). Treat open rate as directional for B2C; CTOR is a more reliable engagement signal.

## How to Interpret Benchmarks

### Above Average
Performing above the industry average means the campaign is competitive, but "above average" alone isn't a target — it depends on spend efficiency. A high CTR with poor conversion rate may indicate clickbait copy or a landing page mismatch.

### Below Average
A metric 20% or more below the industry average warrants investigation. Check:
1. **Targeting** — is the audience too broad or misaligned?
2. **Creative** — is the ad fatigued, unclear, or poorly matched to the channel?
3. **Landing page** — is there a disconnect between the ad promise and the page experience?
4. **Competition** — has the competitive landscape shifted (new entrants, seasonal auction pressure)?

### Thresholds for Action
| Situation | Recommended action |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Metric 10–20% below average | Monitor for one more reporting period; check for external factors |
| Metric 20–40% below average | Investigate root cause; test creative or targeting changes |
| Metric 40%+ below average | Pause and restructure; something fundamental is misaligned |
| Metric declining 3 consecutive periods | Investigate regardless of absolute level — trend matters more than snapshot |

## Seasonal Variation

| Period | Typical Impact |
|--------|---------------|
| January | CPCs drop 15–25% as competition resets budgets. Good time to test. |
| Q1 (B2B) | Decision-makers re-engage after holidays; lead quality improves. |
| March–April | Gradual CPC increase as Q2 budgets activate. |
| Summer (Jun–Aug) | B2B engagement dips 10–15%; B2C varies by sector (travel up, finance down). |
| September | "Back to business" surge in B2B. CPCs rise. |
| October–November | Retail CPCs begin climbing toward Black Friday/Christmas. |
| Black Friday–Christmas | CPMs spike 30–80% across all channels. Non-retail brands may benefit from pausing display and reallocating to search/email. |
| Year-end (B2B) | "Use it or lose it" budget spending inflates auction prices in December. |

Plan budgets and benchmarks seasonally. Comparing December display CPMs to March CPMs without adjustment leads to false conclusions.

## B2B vs B2C Benchmark Differences

| Dimension | B2B | B2C |
|-----------|-----|-----|
| Sales cycle | Weeks to months — optimise for lead quality, not immediate conversion | Minutes to days — optimise for conversion rate and ROAS |
| Typical CPA | £30–£150+ (justified by higher LTV) | £5–£40 |
| CTR (search) | 2.5–4.0% (niche keywords, high intent) | 3.5–5.5% (broader keywords, more competition) |
| CTR (social) | 0.4–0.8% (LinkedIn-heavy) | 1.0–2.0% (Meta/TikTok-heavy) |
| Email open rate | Higher (20–25%) — smaller, engaged lists | Lower (15–22%) — larger, more varied lists |
| Key metric | Cost per qualified lead, pipeline value | ROAS, cost per acquisition, repeat purchase rate |
| Attribution | Multi-touch, long window (30–90 days) | Last-click or short window (7–14 days) often sufficient |

## Performance Trend Indicators

### Improvement Signals
- CTR rising while CPA holds steady or falls (better targeting *and* conversion)
- Quality Score increasing (ad relevance improving)
- Conversion rate rising with consistent traffic volume (not just cherry-picking)
- Engagement rate climbing on newer creatives (audience resonance)
- Cost per lead declining quarter-over-quarter on similar spend

### Decline Signals
- CTR falling while impressions hold (creative fatigue or audience saturation)
- CPA rising with flat conversion rate (increasing competition or auction pressure)
- Rising CPM with flat engagement (diminishing returns on current audience)
- Unsubscribe rate climbing send-over-send (list fatigue or relevance issue)
- View-through conversions falling (brand recall weakening)

### When Trends Conflict
If CTR is rising but conversion rate is falling, the ad is attracting *more* clicks from *less qualified* people — the targeting or creative is broadening beyond the ideal audience. Tighten audience parameters or make the ad's qualifying criteria more explicit.
